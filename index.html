The Cut off for Lasu remains as 190. which implies that all aspirants of the Institution must have at least 190 in the last conducted UTME, before they would be allowed to proceed for the post utme screening

LASU HAS TWO MODE OF ENTRY FOR UTME CANDIDATES: in 2019, LASU introduced a programme for entry into LASU called STREAM 2, it was indeed the beginning of a remarkable era in LASU history. This mode on activation rendered her former entry mode STREAM 1 thereby making entry to LASU for UTME candidates 2 modes. What differs the STREAM 2 from that of the latter is that it offers her student two certificate on graduation, one for the candidates chosen course and the other for an enterpreneural skill the candidate must have chosen on entry. This enterpreneural skills are over 70 including Photography, Web designing and digital marketing, Event planning, Cake and pastries, Shoe making and many more. Reports shows that a students who gets admitted via this platform has the licence to choose different enterpreneural courses of her choice per session. Sounds interesting you know😄 However, it should be noted that there's is difference in her fees. While stream 1 school fees is N57,048.50, that of stream 2 is N150,000 per session (year). Candidates are given the licence to choose either of the stream upon registration. P.S: YOU ARE NOT ALLOWED TO CHOOSE THE BOTH STREAMS AT A TIME.(Visit LASU Admission portal through the link in the nav bar for more information on this). It's also pertinent you note that *upon Admission via stream 2, the validity of the Admission is on a first come, first served basis. The stream 2 platform has limited slots and based on the traffic it usually drives, the university retains the validity of the admission of only those who pay 75% of their fees alongside the Medial and acceptance fee, this makes up N105,000. Failure to pay on time will lead to forfeiture of the candidate's admission.

    Here is the formula to calculating LASU aggregate 🔥 The first thing is you divide your JAMB score by 8 E.g 250÷8= 31.25 And for your o level grades here is how you grade them. A1... 10 points B2... 9 points B3... 8 points C4... 7 points C5... 6 points C6... 5 points Now let's assume you scored 250 in JAMB and you have this o level result here 📌 Mathematics... B2 (8p) English... A1 (10p) Physics... A1 (10p) Chemistry... B2 (8p) Biology.. C4 (7p) Now to get your final aggregate score simply ADD YOUR JAMB AGGREGATE WITH YOUR O LEVEL AGGREGATE as we have it below 🔽 31.25+8+10+10+8+7= 74.25 Therefore, 74.25 will be your official admission screening aggregate. And mind you, admission is based on general performance i.e the higher the aggregate the higher your chance of admission ✅

    LASU does not have a precise departmental cut off mark and this is because their admission is based on general performance. So, the higher your aggregate the higher your chance of securing admission ahead of your peers. This is why we have the merit list, supplementary list and the succeeding lists (called batches). Your admission is determined by the relevance of your aggregate.

    Additionally, indigenous candidates have about 20% chance in favor of them. For example, an Indigene with 70% aggregate may be admitted for a course a non indigene is unable to secure with a 74% aggregate.

    Note that your admission is not determined by your JAMB score only. A candidate with 192 may secure admission ahead of a candidate with 290 (know that).
